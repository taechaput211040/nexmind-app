# NEXMIND Character Design Prompt Bank

## Master Art Direction

Create multiple original anime character designs for NEXMIND in a consistent modern shonen anime concept-art style. These are futuristic arcane guild members: AI agents embodied as techno-mages in a future magical company.

Core style: modern shonen anime character concept art, sharp angular line art, stylized semi-realistic anime proportions, expressive intense eyes, bold cel shading, clean high-contrast shadows, dynamic but readable silhouettes, detailed costume design, streetwear fantasy aesthetic, game character design quality, clean white or light neutral background.

Line and shape language: crisp confident linework, angular facial features, sharp eyes, defined jawline, stylized hair shapes, slim athletic body types, strong silhouettes, clean clothing folds, graphic costume shapes, asymmetrical outfit details, tactical belts, cloaks, rune circuitry, cyber-magic clothing, neon accent trims.

Color and rendering: vivid but controlled palettes, flat anime colors with cel-shaded shadows, minimal soft gradients, high readability, polished 2D anime concept art. Use a consistent NEXMIND palette: obsidian black, deep graphite, electric cyan, violet arcane glow, gold command glyphs, and each department accent color.

Design direction: every character belongs to the same anime/game universe, but each needs a unique silhouette, hairstyle, artifact or weapon, outfit theme, personality, pose language, and accent color. Avoid copying any existing character, costume, artist, or copyrighted style.

Global negative prompt: low quality, blurry, photorealistic, 3D render, western comic style, overly muscular superhero body, cute chibi style, mascot, copied character, copyrighted costume, messy sketch, messy anatomy, extra limbs, unreadable face, low detail, soft moe style, realistic skin texture, old-school anime style, watercolor, oil painting, painterly rendering, cluttered background, heavy gore, realistic weapon violence, generic fantasy armor, medieval-only design.

Recommended format: vertical character sheet, 4:5 or 2:3 ratio, full body centered in front-facing or 3/4 view, one optional headshot inset, one artifact or weapon detail inset, small handwritten-style design notes, clean white or light neutral background.

## Shared Visual Rules

- Every member wears a black graphite base outfit with one strong department accent.
- Each character needs one signature artifact that clearly explains their work.
- Use rune-circuit motifs instead of ordinary fantasy symbols.
- Use bold cel shading and high-contrast shadow shapes; avoid painterly rendering.
- Keep the silhouette readable even if the costume has many details.
- Keep faces humanlike and stylish, not robotic, except AUTO.
- ARIA should feel central and ceremonial; other agents should feel specialized.
- Do not make all outfits identical: vary hood, coat, armor, robe, scout gear, mask, cape, hair shape, and artifact.

## Department Accent Guide

- Secretary: violet prism glow, command sigils, orbiting routing glyphs.
- Dev Forge: cyan circuitry, tools, blueprints, terminal fragments.
- Design: magenta/pink light, ink ribbons, moon/flower geometry.
- Content: warm gold, paper scrolls, broadcast charms, manuscript sparks.
- Trading: emerald and red market marks, gold-dragon linework, chart glyphs.
- Intelligence: blue-violet grids, maps, memory lanterns, encrypted locks.
- Finance: amber/mint ledger light, coins, contract seals.
- Systems: crimson/cyan warning light, law chains, prototype rings, voice waves.
